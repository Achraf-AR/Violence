So What is Violence?

Violence in shourt:

Violence, that dark specter haunting our society, manifests in countless ways. It can be brutal and physical, psychological, or verbal. Together, we must work to eradicate this darkness and build a more peaceful and cooperative community.

violence in detail:

Violence typically refers to the intentional use of physical force or power, threatened or actual, against oneself, another person, or a group or community, that either results in or has a high likelihood of resulting in injury, death, psychological harm, maldevelopment, or deprivation. It can manifest in various forms such as physical violence, sexual violence, emotional or psychological violence, and structural violence. Violence can occur at various levels including interpersonal violence (between individuals), collective violence (between groups or communities), and structural violence (embedded within social structures and institutions).

What is The Types of Violence ?

A picture of institutional violence

Violence institutionnelle : Cela fait référence à des politiques, des pratiques ou des comportements au sein de l'institution universitaire qui peuvent contribuer à la marginalisation, à la discrimination ou à l'exclusion de certains groupes d'étudiants ou de membres du personnel.

Harcèlement verbal ou intimidation

Cela comprend les comportements verbaux agressifs, les menaces, les insultes, les brimades ou d'autres formes de comportement intimidant qui peuvent créer un climat d'insécurité ou de peur pour les membres de la communauté universitaire. Violence physique : Tout acte de violence physique, tel que les agressions physiques, les bagarres ou les attaques physiques, qui peuvent se produire sur le campus ou lors d'événements universitaires.

Cyberintimidation ou cyberharcèlement

Ce type de violence implique l'utilisation de la technologie, comme les médias sociaux, les messages électroniques ou les forums en ligne, pour harceler, intimider ou menacer d'autres personnes de manière répétée.

what is the causes of violence ?

Understanding the factors that contribute to violence within educational settings is crucial for prevention and intervention. School violence encompasses various forms of aggression, occurring on school premises, during travel to and from school, and even at school-related events.
The causes of school violence are multifaceted and often interconnected. Here are some risk factors that can make a child more likely to engage in violent behavior:

Bullying

Being a victim of bullying can lead to feelings of anger and retaliation, resulting in violent acts.

Family Environment

A dysfunctional family, exposure to domestic violence, or lack of parental support can impact a student’s behavior.

Mental health conditions

Conditions like depression, anxiety, or conduct disorders can influence behavior and emotional regulation.

Substance abuse

Alcohol or drug use can impair judgment and increase the likelihood of aggressive behavior.

Access to weapons

Easy access to weapons increases the risk of violent incidents in schools.
